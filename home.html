Blood cannot be manufactured – it can only come from generous donors.
Type O-negative blood (red cells) can be transfused to patients of all blood types. It is always in great demand and often in short supply. Type AB-positive plasma can be transfused to patients of all other blood types. AB plasma is also usually in short supply


Blood donations in India are conducted by several organizations and hospitals by organizing blood donation camps.
Donors can also visit blood banks in hospitals to donate blood or directly to a receiver.
Despite the shortage of donated blood, efforts by the government and various organizations have
led to a decrease in the demand and supply gap over the years.
The number of voluntary blood donors increased from 54.4% in 2006–2007 to 83.1% in 2011–2012, with the number of blood units increasing from 4.4 million units in 2006–2007 to 9.3 million units in 2012–2013.
